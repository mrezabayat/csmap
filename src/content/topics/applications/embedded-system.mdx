## In simple terms

An **embedded system** is a computer that doesn't look like a computer. It's the chip inside your washing machine, your fridge, your car, your toothbrush — bound to a specific task, often with tight constraints on memory, power, and timing.

## More detail

Spectrum from small to large:

- **Microcontroller (MCU)** — a single chip with CPU + RAM + flash + peripherals. KBs of memory, no OS, runs one program. Arduino, STM32, ESP32.
- **Microprocessor + minimal OS** — a Linux board (Raspberry Pi, BeagleBone, Yocto-built device).
- **Real-time OS (RTOS)** — FreeRTOS, Zephyr, VxWorks, QNX. Guarantees timing.
- **High-end embedded** — automotive ECUs, avionics, medical equipment; often safety-critical with rigorous certification (DO-178C, ISO 26262).

Characteristic constraints:

- **Resource-bound**: KBs of RAM, MB of flash; no luxury of large libraries.
- **Power-bound**: battery- or solar-powered; must sleep most of the time.
- **Real-time**: a missed deadline is a failure, not a warning.
- **Reliability**: deployed devices may never be touched again for a decade.
- **No graceful failure**: a hung thermostat in winter is a serious bug.

Programming is dominated by C and C++; Rust is rising, especially in safety-critical work. Higher-level languages (MicroPython, TinyGo) appear on bigger devices.

## Why it matters

Most computers in the world are embedded — by orders of magnitude. A modern car has 50–100; a smart home, dozens. They are the invisible substrate of the modern physical world.

## Real-world examples

- An Arduino blinking an LED is a learning embedded system.
- A car's anti-lock braking system is a hard-real-time embedded controller.
- A SpaceX Falcon 9 has hundreds of embedded computers cooperating to land the booster.

- The James Webb Space Telescope's onboard computer runs a SPARC-derived chip at 200 MHz with 12 GB of storage — a tiny resource budget for a $10 billion instrument.

## Common misconceptions

- **"Embedded means weak."** A modern Tesla has more compute on board than many laptops.
- **"Embedded developers don't need to care about software engineering."** They need to care more: bugs are recall-level events, not patch-on-Tuesday events.
